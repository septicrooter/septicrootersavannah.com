FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is a septic system and how does it work?
A septic system is an underground wastewater treatment structure used where there's no centralized sewer. Wastewater flows into a watertight tank where solids settle and bacteria break them down. The clarified effluent then flows into a drain field where it's filtered through the soil. Regular inspection and maintenance prevent costly failures.
Do I need a permit for septic work in this area?
Septic permits in Georgia are issued through county Environmental Health offices under Georgia EPD. In South Carolina, permits are issued through SC DHEC. SepticRooter‚Ñ¢ handles the permit process on both sides of the state line. SepticRooter‚Ñ¢ is licensed and handles permit applications and required inspections for you.
What are typical septic soil conditions in this area?
The Savannah and Low Country region features sandy coastal plain soils with high water tables ‚Äî very different from Georgia's piedmont clay. High water tables require careful site evaluation and often mandate alternative or mound systems near tidal areas, wetlands, and the coast. Proper system selection is critical in this environment. We evaluate your specific site before recommending any system type or repair approach.
How often should I pump my septic tank?
Most homeowners should pump every 3‚Äì5 years to prevent backups and extend the system's life. Household size, water usage, and whether you have a garbage disposal all affect the timeline.
